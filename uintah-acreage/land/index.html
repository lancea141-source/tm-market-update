Scope: Tight band for valuing a specific acreage parcel. Only parcels between 2 and 15 acres are modeled.

What the data shows

Size elasticity of 0.260. Doubling lot size raises price about 20%, so price per acre falls roughly 40% with each doubling. Buyers pay for the right to build, not for raw ground.
489 of 726 sales closed below their original asking price, median discount 12.7%. Sale-to-last-list ratios near 100% are an artifact of Utah non-disclosure — they measure price after reductions.
Current inventory is priced +21.2% against the model. Of 99 actives in scope, 54 are more than 15% above modeled value.

Inside the modeled band price follows size, location and services predictably. Above it, parcels price on entitlement risk and utility distance instead. If you own acreage that has not sold, that is usually the reason, and it is fixable.

Method

Log-linear regression on 210 sales, Jul 2023 to Jun 2026, parcels 2–15 acres. Predictors: lot size (log), city, utility availability, months elapsed, and 6 lot features. R² = 0.604. Median absolute error 30.0%. 6 outliers removed at 2.5× IQR. Source: WFRMLS/URE closed sales.
